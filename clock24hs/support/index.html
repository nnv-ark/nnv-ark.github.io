Clock24h — Support

A minimalist 24-hour analog watch face whose day and night arcs follow your actual sunrise and sunset.

Getting started

  • On first launch, Clock24h asks for location while in use. Tap Allow so the dial can compute today's sunrise and sunset for where you are. If you decline, Clock24h still works and falls back to Reykjavík.
  • Tap the dial to switch between color themes — Classic (white day, black night, red accents) and Dusk (warm amber day, slate-blue night, with date and city). Your choice is remembered between launches.
  • Noon (12) sits at the top, midnight (0) at the bottom. The hour hand makes one full rotation per day instead of two.
  • The light (day) and dark (night) arcs are not fixed at 6 and 18 — they track your real sunrise and sunset and grow or shrink with the season.

Common questions

The city on the dial is wrong, or just says REYKJAVÍK. Clock24h reverse-geocodes through Apple's MapKit. The first lookup happens after location is granted; if you've just installed the app, give it a moment after launch. If it stays on the fallback, check Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Clock24h is set to While Using the App.

The whole dial is one color in midsummer or midwinter. That's correct. Above the Arctic Circle the sun can stay up for weeks (midnight sun — whole dial in day color) or stay down for weeks (polar night — whole dial in night color). Clock24h handles both gracefully.

The second hand looks like it's missing for a moment. The minute and second hands cross paths at every minute mark. For one second per minute they overlap.
