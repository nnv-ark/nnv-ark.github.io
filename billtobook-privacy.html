Bill To Book — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 5 June 2026

Bill To Book is designed to keep your documents on your own device. We do not run servers that receive your data, and we do not collect, store, sell, or share any personal information.

What the app does with your data

When you scan a bill or document, the captured images are processed entirely on your device to create a PDF. That PDF is then handed to Apple's built-in Mail composer so you can send it from your own email account. The document is only sent when you tap Send. We never see it.

Information we do not collect

  • No account or sign-in is required.
  • No analytics, advertising, or tracking technologies are used.
  • No scans, PDFs, email addresses, or company details leave your device except through the email you personally send.
  • We do not use third-party data collection SDKs.

Information stored on your device

Company names, recipient email addresses, sender identities, and receipt counters that you enter are stored locally on your device so the app can work. This information stays on your device (and in your own iCloud/device backups, if you use them) and is removed when you delete the app.

Camera

The app requests camera access only to scan documents. Captured images are used solely to build the PDF you choose to send and are not transmitted to us.

Children's privacy

Bill To Book is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
